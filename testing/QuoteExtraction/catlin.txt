Community Arts Program Summer Camps 2026 Summer Camps 2026 Catalog Summer Camps FAQ Mobile Menu Toggle Home > Support Catlin Gabel > Endowment Endowment Catlin Gabel’s $41M endowment was built over decades, with support from families, alumni, employees, and friends. The school’s long-term health depends on the endowment, which is why it is one of our Eagles Soar campaign priorities. An endowed fund can be established in honor of an individual, a particular interest, or it can be unrestricted to support the school’s greatest needs. We ask for a minimum of $100,000 to establish an endowment fund; subsequent gifts of any amount can be made thereafter. This level of support allows for adequate annual support of programs and initiatives that our endowments aim to impact. Most Common Areas for Endowed Support Financial Assistance for Students Professional Development for Teachers Experiential Learning Through Global & Outdoor Education Athletics Head of School Priorities Financial Assistance for Students Need-based financial assistance is available to offset the cost of tuition, books, laptops, school trips, bus service, and the lunchroom. Creating an endowed fund for financial assistance is one of the best ways to make a Catlin Gabel education more affordable for eligible students now and into the future. Professional Development for Teachers An ongoing commitment to professional growth is expected of those who work at Catlin Gabel. You can help us continue this long tradition of teaching excellence through an endowed fund for professional development. Experiential Learning Through Global & Outdoor Education Catlin Gabel’s Global and Outdoor Education Programs seek to engage and challenge our students to become active stewards and citizens of the world. Global Education provides cross-cultural experiences for students, while Outdoor Education offers avenues for students to learn and grow outside the classroom. Establishing an endowed fund in support of either of these efforts will ensure these experiences will be available to students for years to come. Athletics We value the life lessons taught by athletics participation: teamwork, overcoming adversity, selflessness, and strength of character. Our no-cut policy, competitive spirit, and exceptional coaches and facilities, make our athletic program one of the best in the state. Head of School Priorities Each year, the Head of School and the Senior Leadership Team set curricular and operational priorities for the school. Enacting these priorities would not be possible without donor support. If you have other ideas for your donation, we would love to hear about it! For more information, please contact Nicole Rinetti-Clawson, Chief Advancement Officer at 503-297-1894 ext.5010.
